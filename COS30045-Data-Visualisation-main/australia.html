Visualisation 1 - Amount of Immigrants in Canada from 2016 to 2021

In Australia, immigration experienced a decline from 2017 to 2020, mainly due to the government's stricter migration policies implemented since the 2010s. This followed a period of significant immigration in the 2000s and 1990s. The introduction of the "Skilled Occupations List" focused on STEM professions aimed to address labor shortages in science and technology. Occupations not included in the list faced challenges in obtaining permanent residency. The majority of immigrants to Australia are skilled workers, with fewer refugees or economic seekers.
Surprisingly, despite the pandemic, Australia witnessed an increase in immigration in 2021. There was also a rise in the proportion of "family immigrants." This can be attributed to relaxed migration policies by Australian states, including expanding the "Skilled Occupations List" to stimulate the economy. However, the number of refugees and individuals seeking assistance remained relatively small. Australia's immigration policies demonstrate a pragmatic approach.
Overall, Australia's immigration landscape reflects changing policies and priorities, with a focus on skilled workers and economic recovery, while maintaining a measured approach to refugee intake.

Visualisation 2 - Changes in Imigration and Economic statistic from 2016 to 2021 (Australia)

Before COVID-19 Australia’s economic indexes also exhibited constant trends. The GDP growth declined and the unemployment rate grew in 2020 but then returned to its average level. In fact, the unemployment rate even reached its nadir in 5 years’ time (around 4%). The surge in the immigrant population might play a big role in this successful economic recovery. In other words, the relaxation of Australian immigration policies was effective in protecting the Australian economy from the negative impacts of COVID-19.
